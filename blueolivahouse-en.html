Project
Understanding

Blue Olive House is currently a rental property, with most bookings coming from Booking.com and a smaller share from Airbnb. Relying exclusively on OTAs comes with specific costs:

Booking commission

15–18% of every booking goes to the platform, not to you

No brand identity

The guest forgets who you are; next time they "search on Booking" again

Zero repeat business

No channel for direct communication, loyalty, or returning customers

Loss of narrative control

Photos, descriptions, value proposition — everything filtered by OTA standards

SEO that goes elsewhere

When someone googles "Blue Olive House," they find Booking, not you

No upsell opportunity

You can't pitch extras (transfer, breakfast, late check-out) at your own pace

From property to platform

The requested solution is not a static site for a single property. It is a hospitality platform with multi-listing architecture from day one. Practically, this means:

  • "Property page" structure with a dynamic template, so every new property gets its own page automatically
  • A browse / search page with filters (location, dates, guests, features)
  • Admin / CMS where you or Hosthub can add a new property: photos, description, amenities, pricing, link to Hosthub feed
  • Adding a future property is a matter of minutes via the admin, not developer work

The logic: every direct booking through your own platform saves 15–18% in OTA commission. With one property the savings are significant; with ten properties the platform becomes an asset, not an expense. The goal is not to replace Booking & Airbnb, but to add a third, owned channel that scales with you.

Hosthub
Integration

Hosthub is already your central management tool. It works as a channel manager: when a booking comes in via Booking.com, Hosthub automatically blocks those dates on every other channel (Airbnb, etc.), preventing double bookings. This significantly changes the architecture of our platform:

We don't need to build our own channel manager. Hosthub already handles synchronization between Booking, Airbnb and the rest of the OTAs. We simply talk to Hosthub, and Hosthub keeps the channels updated.

Sync flow (Option B, with booking engine)

1
Availability from Hosthub to the site Our platform pulls each property's availability calendar from Hosthub (via iCal feed or API). This way the site always shows truly available dates, regardless of whether the booking came in through Booking, Airbnb or anywhere else.
2
Direct booking on the site The guest selects dates, completes checkout and pays by card via Stripe or Viva Wallet.
3
Booking from site back to Hosthub The new booking is exposed in turn via an iCal feed (or pushed via API) so that Hosthub picks it up and blocks those dates on Booking, Airbnb & the rest of the channels.
4
All channels stay in sync Whether the booking comes from Booking, Airbnb or your own site, all three channels are updated automatically within minutes. Zero risk of double bookings.

Note: the exact integration method (iCal vs API) depends on your Hosthub plan and the capabilities they expose to their customers. At kickoff we'll have a technical call with Hosthub to choose the optimal route. Either way, both methods are well-supported by Hosthub.

  • Hosthub: subscription cost stays independent and is paid directly to them

Discussion points

The points below need decisions before kickoff. They don't affect today's proposal, but will be finalized at the discovery call:

  • Brand & platform name: will the platform carry the "Blue Olive House" brand (with new properties listed under it), or do you want a separate parent brand that hosts Blue Olive House as the first listing?
  • Domain: new domain for the platform or use of an existing one? Recommendation: a separate .com domain from the start.
  • Hosthub plan & technical capabilities: does your current Hosthub plan include API access or only iCal feeds? This determines whether sync is real-time (API) or 5–15 min latency (iCal). Both work.
  • Property management: will new properties be added by you, or do you want Hosthub to also have access to the CMS? If yes, with what permissions?
  • Payment policy (B): full online payment, or 30–50% deposit with the balance on-site / before check-in?
  • Cancellation policy: what is the default policy (flexible / moderate / strict)? Will it differ per property?
  • Pricing: will we work with fixed seasonal prices, or do you want pricing also synced with Hosthub (if your plan supports it)?
  • Additional languages: beyond EL/EN, should we plan for others (DE, FR, IT) for foreign markets?
  • Legal: Terms of Use, Privacy Policy, Cookies. Do you have a legal advisor or do you need a recommendation?
  • Tax (Option B): Hosthub has tax automation for Greek hosts (myDATA, AADE). Will the new direct channel be folded into the same flow or handled separately?

All of the above are decided together at the discovery call. You don't need answers now.

Implementation
Methodology

We follow a structured, transparent process. No surprises: you always know what stage you're in and what's left. To move to each next step, the previous one must be completed and approved.

  • 1
    Kickoff & Discovery: in-depth discussion of the brand, aesthetic style, target audience, the discussion points from this proposal, and a technical call with Hosthub (Option B only) to define the integration method. Asset gathering: photos, logo, descriptions, location data.
  • 2
    Wireframes & UX flow: wireframes for the homepage, listings page, property page template, and admin / CMS. For Option B: special focus on the booking flow and admin dashboard. First approval. If a week passes with no remarks, the wireframes are considered accepted.
  • 3
    Visual Design: full UI design in Figma or directly in code with live preview. Up to 2 rounds of revisions based on feedback, within 2 weeks of each presentation.
  • 4
    Development: front-end and back-end build. CMS setup and importing the first property (Blue Olive House). For Option B: Hosthub integration, Stripe/Viva setup, admin dashboard. Weekly progress updates.
  • 5
    QA & Testing: testing on Chrome, Firefox, Edge, Safari, desktop, tablet, mobile. For Option B: end-to-end booking test, payment test, Hosthub sync test (booking on the site → blocked dates appear in Booking via Hosthub), webhooks.
  • 6
    Delivery & Training: launch, training on CMS & admin dashboard, documentation for adding new properties by you or Hosthub. 1 month of free bug fixing after delivery.*

* The delivery date is the date we hand over the platform for review, not the go-live date. Delays on your side (asset delivery, feedback, Hosthub setup) affect the estimated timeline.

Technology

We don't use WordPress. We build custom on Next.js 16 + TypeScript + Tailwind on the frontend, with Sanity CMS for property & content management, Supabase (PostgreSQL with Row Level Security) for the bookings & users database, Stripe / Viva for payments, Resend for transactional emails, and hosting on Vercel. The Hosthub integration runs through iCal feeds or its own API. This means speed, security and low long-term running cost, with the ability to scale to dozens of properties without architectural changes.
